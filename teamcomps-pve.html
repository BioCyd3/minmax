PvE Team Composition Guide

PvE Team Building Principles

Player vs. Environment (PvE) content demands teams that can sustain themselves through long fights, clear waves of enemies efficiently, and overcome powerful bosses. Unlike PvP, which often favors pure burst damage, PvE rewards strategic depth, survivability, and synergy.

Survivability is Key

Tanks that protect the whole team (like Statchew) and clutch healers/shielders (like Platyputz) are invaluable for surviving tough boss mechanics and attrition.

AoE for Trash Mobs

Efficiently clearing waves of weaker enemies is crucial. Pals with reliable Area of Effect (AoE) skills save time and protect your team from being overwhelmed.

Synergy > Raw Power

A well-constructed mono-element team built around a buffer like Cerverdant (for Earth) or Baboom will often outperform a random assortment of powerful URs.

Control the Fight

Crowd Control (CC) like Stun (from Dolphriend or Hoofrit) and Freeze (from Snowkami) are just as critical in PvE to interrupt powerful boss attacks and disable dangerous elite enemies.

Team Composition Tiers

The following team compositions are ranked by their effectiveness in PvE content, considering both power level and accessibility for different player investment levels.

S-Tier: Meta Dominance

These teams represent the absolute pinnacle of PvE performance, featuring multiple UR Pals with game-changing passives and synergies.

A-Tier: Specialized Excellence

Focused strategies that excel in specific content types or leverage powerful mono-element synergies for consistent performance.

B-Tier: Practical Efficiency

Resource-focused teams designed for farming and daily activities, balancing combat effectiveness with utility benefits.

Team 1: The Indomitable Fortress (Generalist)

This team is the pinnacle of "good stuff" composition. It leverages the two most powerful team-wide passives in the game to create a massive statistical advantage before the fight even begins. It's incredibly durable, cripples enemy defenses, and has top-tier control and damage to handle any situation.

Front Row (2)

  • Statchew (UR)Role: Main Tank, Team Damage Reduction
  • Barkplug (UR)Role: Off-Tank, Team DEF Shred

Back Row (3)

  • Abuzzinian (UR)Role: Dodge Tank, Multi-Target CC
  • Lucidina (UR)Role: Elite Single-Target Deletion
  • Hoofrit (UR)Role: Backline Assassin, Stun

Strategy Breakdown

Statchew's 'Sentinel' passive reduces all incoming damage to the front row by 20%, while Barkplug's 'Ion Disruption' passive reduces all enemy DEF by 20%. This combo makes your team both incredibly tanky and hit significantly harder. Hoofrit immediately pressures the enemy backline. Lucidina targets and eliminates the single most dangerous threat. Abuzzinian survives with high evasion and locks down multiple enemies with Paralysis, preventing them from using their Rage Skills. It's a symphony of overwhelming power and control.

Budget / Alternative Options

This is a "whale" team. To replicate its function on a budget:

  • Replace Statchew with Platyputz (SSR) for his personal tankiness and clutch shield.
  • Replace Barkplug with a durable debuffer like Axollium (SSR). You lose the powerful DEF shred, but gain an Accuracy debuff.
  • Replace Lucidina with Squeezel (SSR) or Bruiseberry (SSR) for strong single-target burst.
  • Replace Hoofrit with Snowkami (SSR) to focus on single-target assassination with Freeze instead of backline pressure.

Team 2: The Mono-Earth Quake Squad

This composition focuses on the powerful synergies available to Earth-type Pals. It's a slower, more methodical team that becomes an unstoppable grinding machine, boasting incredible durability and ramping damage thanks to its powerful, stacking buffs.

Front Row (2)

  • Statchew (UR)Role: Main Tank, Team-wide DR
  • Baboom (UR)Role: Leader, DPS, Stun

Back Row (3)

  • Cerverdant (SSR)Role: CORE BUFF SUPPORT
  • Terrastudo (SSR)Role: AoE Damage
  • Axollium (SSR)Role: AoE Damage, Debuff

Strategy Breakdown

This team is all about buffs. Baboom provides a passive +15% Attack to all Earth allies just for being in the lineup. Cerverdant's Rage Skill gives a temporary but massive +30% Attack buff to all Earth allies. These two buffs stack, turning even the SSRs into damage powerhouses. Statchew holds the line, making the front nearly invincible. Terrastudo and Axollium provide the AoE needed to sweep through enemy formations.

Budget / Alternative Options

Cerverdant is the irreplaceable core of this team.

  • Replace Statchew with another tough Earth Pal, though no one replicates his team-wide passive. Platyputz (SSR) is a good generic tank replacement.
  • If you lack Baboom, move Terrastudo to the front row and add another Earth DPS like Bruiseberry (SSR) to the back.
  • Any Earth SSR can fill the DPS slots if you are missing Terrastudo or Axollium. The key is to have Earth Pals to benefit from the buffs.

Team 3: The Resource Maximizer (Farming)

This team isn't designed for the absolute hardest PvE content. Instead, its purpose is to maximize your resource gains from daily activities like clearing the map of Bullymon and completing Intel Quests. It's a long-term investment team that accelerates your camp's growth.

Front Row (2)

  • Platyputz (SSR)Role: Tank, Survivability
  • Blazeal (UR)Role: AoE Damage, Fast Clearing

Back Row (3)

  • Meowdame (SSR)Role: CORE - Bullymon Loot Bonus
  • Incineraptor (SSR)Role: CORE - Intel Quest Bonus
  • Magmolin (UR)Role: Damage & Global Steel Buff

Strategy Breakdown

The strategy is simple: win fights while earning more loot. Meowdame's passive grants 75% extra Gold, Lumber, and Steel from Bullymon. Incineraptor's passive provides bonus treasure from Intel Quests. These two are the reason to run the team. The other three slots are filled with Pals who are both strong in combat and provide excellent passive benefits. Platyputz ensures the team can auto-battle without dying. Blazeal provides fast AoE clears. Magmolin is a powerhouse fighter who also provides a 100% global boost to your Steel production, making him the ultimate multi-tasker.

Budget / Alternative Options

The core of this team, Meowdame and Incineraptor, are SSRs and thus fairly accessible. The other slots are flexible.

  • Replace Blazeal or Magmolin with any strong AoE damage dealer you have, like Terrastudo (SSR) or Fingenue (UR).
  • The tank slot can be filled by any durable Pal. Statchew (UR) is a luxury upgrade.
  • Fill empty slots with other Pals who have valuable camp passives, like Cerverdant (SSR) for his logging skill or Bruiseberry (SSR) for his training skill.

Building Your Own Teams

While these compositions represent proven strategies, the best team is one that fits your playstyle and available Pals. Use these examples as templates, but don't be afraid to experiment with substitutions based on your roster.

Start with Core Synergies

Identify 2-3 Pals that work well together, then build around them. A strong core is better than five random powerful Pals.

Consider Your Content

Different PvE content may require different approaches. Boss fights need more control, while farming benefits from AoE and speed.

Upgrade Gradually

Start with budget versions using SSR Pals, then gradually upgrade to UR versions as you acquire them. The team structure remains the same.

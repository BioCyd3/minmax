NAP 20: The Truth

No spin. No sugar. This is the raw truth about the cartel that ruins competitive strategy games from the inside out.

What is NAP 20?

A Non-Aggression Pact among the top 20 alliances in a server. It means:

  • No attacks between the elites.
  • Full coordination on events, zones, and rankings.
  • Complete power lockout for all other alliances.

It’s not peace. It’s a monopoly.

Where This Has Happened Before

  • Rise of Kingdoms (RoK): Kingdoms often had massive NAP agreements between the top 3–5 alliances. Result? Dead kingdoms, mass migration, enforced farm zones.
  • Game of War: Early kingdoms had 20+ alliances form “Royal NAPs” to lock down events. Whales sat at the top. Everyone else bled out or quit.
  • Lords Mobile: Guild coalitions enforced cross-guild NAPs that prevented real war until KvK. Result? Only one hour of action every two weeks.
  • Evony: The King’s Return: NAPs between top alliances turned entire servers into AFK farms. Servers were restarted or bled dry of competition.

Every single time, the pattern is the same: NAP → stagnation → collapse.

The Real Reasons Behind NAP 20

  • Fear of whales fighting each other.
  • Desire to farm uninterrupted.
  • Top alliances don’t want churn—they want obedience.

It’s not about protecting the server. It’s about controlling it.

The Sales Pitch vs Reality

Pitch: “We’re protecting new players.”
Reality: You’re herding them into resource zones and telling them not to PvP.

Pitch: “We’re preventing toxic drama.”
Reality: You’ve replaced drama with silence and made the game boring.

Pitch: “We’ll all grow stronger together.”
Reality: Only the top 5 grow. The rest stagnate or get kicked out when they fall behind.

The Lifecycle of a NAP Server

  1. Server launches. War is chaotic and raw.
  2. Whales get tired of risk. NAP 10 becomes NAP 20.
  3. Top alliances divide the map and stop fighting each other.
  4. Mid-tier alliances get crushed if they refuse to submit.
  5. Activity drops. Growth slows. Players quit. Events become scripted.
  6. Eventually, someone breaks the NAP. Civil war erupts. Server either resets... or dies.

Symptoms of NAP Disease

  • “No kill zone” rules.
  • Pre-scheduled wars only between non-NAP alliances.
  • Fake PvP during events. Real coordination behind the scenes.
  • Leadership council drama disguised as diplomacy.
  • Whales dictating what “fun” looks like for everyone else.

How to Break It

  • Find the weak links. One inactive alliance inside the NAP can shatter trust.
  • Expose hypocrisy: leaked DMs, favoritism, secret attacks.
  • Unite the non-NAP alliances. Target one NAP elite and burn them relentlessly.
  • Force escalation. Force chaos. Once blood spills, the spell breaks.
  • Make war inevitable again.

Final Word
